📊 How the Route Optimizer Works

The route optimizer uses a multi-criteria scoring system to recommend the best commute routes based on your historical riding data.

🎯 Scoring Methodology

Each route is evaluated using three key factors, weighted to balance speed, efficiency, and safety:

⏱️

Time Score

Weight: 40%

What it measures: How fast you can complete the route

Calculation: Faster routes score higher. We also consider consistency - routes with less time variation score better.

Formula: Normalized average duration + consistency bonus

📏

Distance Score

Weight: 30%

What it measures: Route efficiency and directness

Calculation: Shorter routes generally score higher, but we balance this with time - sometimes a slightly longer route is faster.

Formula: Normalized distance (inverted - shorter is better)

🛡️

Safety Score

Weight: 30%

What it measures: Route familiarity and predictability

Calculation: Routes you use more frequently score higher (more familiar = safer). Flatter routes with less elevation change also score better.

Formula: Usage frequency + elevation consistency

🧮 Final Score Calculation

Overall Score = (Time Score × 0.40) + (Distance Score × 0.30) + (Safety Score × 0.30)

All scores are normalized to a 0-100 scale, then weighted and combined. Higher scores indicate better routes.

🔍 Route Grouping

Similar routes are automatically grouped together using the Fréchet distance algorithm, which measures how similar two paths are by considering:

  • Path order: Routes must follow similar sequences of turns and streets
  • Spatial proximity: GPS points should be close together along the entire route
  • Direction consistency: Routes going the same direction are grouped separately

Routes with similarity above 85% are grouped together. The most frequently used route in each group becomes the representative route.

🌦️ Weather Integration

Current weather conditions are fetched for each route's start and end points, including:

  • Temperature: Helps you dress appropriately
  • Wind speed & direction: Identifies headwinds and tailwinds
  • Precipitation: Alerts you to rain or snow

Weather data is cached for 90 minutes to minimize API calls while keeping information current.

💡 Tips for Best Results

  • Record at least 5-10 commutes on each route for accurate analysis
  • Use consistent start/end points (within 500m of home/work)
  • Name your activities with keywords like "commute" or "to work" for better filtering
  • The more data you have, the more accurate the recommendations become

🔧 Customization

You can adjust the scoring weights in config/config.yaml to match your priorities:

optimization:
  weights:
    time: 0.4      # Increase if speed is your priority
    distance: 0.3  # Increase if you want shorter routes
    safety: 0.3    # Increase if you prefer familiar routes

💡 How Wind Scoring Works

Our recommendation system analyzes wind conditions along each route and calculates a Wind Score (0-1):

  • 70% weight on second half - Tailwinds when you're tired are more valuable
  • 30% weight on first half - Fighting headwinds when fresh is manageable
  • 10% bonus for routes with strong consistent tailwinds (>0.8 score) on return

Score Guide: 0.80-1.00 = Excellent 0.65-0.79 = Good 0.50-0.64 = Fair 0.00-0.49 = Poor
